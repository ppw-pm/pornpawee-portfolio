01 Nov 2022
// user_research.sh research

Two weeks of semi-structured interviews and naturalistic observation. 10 interviews (remote + in-person) covered lifestyle, diet, grocery habits, environmental awareness, kitchen behaviour and food waste. 6 naturalistic observations — meal prep and grocery shopping — captured real behaviour in context.

14 Nov 2022
// data_synthesis.sh synthesis

Affinity mapping organised findings into clusters. Aggregated empathy mapping then segmented users by shared behaviour — surfacing three archetypes: busy schedules, health conditions, and financial constraints. These became the foundation for three personas.

💡 insights_summary.json — tl;dr from 10 interviews + 6 observations
4 key findings
finding_01 · time
Cooking feels like a burden, not a choice

Participants with demanding work schedules consistently defaulted to takeaway — not because they didn't want to cook, but because the time cost felt too high. They needed a way to make healthy cooking feel achievable on a weeknight.

finding_02 · environment
Sustainable intent blocked by price & access

Users wanted to make eco-friendly food choices but felt priced out. Sustainable options were seen as a luxury. Access to affordable local alternatives was the missing bridge between values and behaviour.

finding_03 · nutrition
Nutrition knowledge exists — follow-through doesn't

People knew what a balanced diet looked like but found it too effortful to track in daily life. The friction wasn't awareness — it was the constant cognitive load of checking and planning every meal.

finding_04 · waste
Food waste is habitual, not deliberate

Participants regularly bought more than they could eat, especially from supermarkets with large-format packaging. Waste happened by default — without the tools or knowledge to manage portions and expiry dates proactively.

These insights clustered into three distinct user archetypes, each shaped by a primary constraint:

Steve
⏱ busy schedule

Working professional who relies on takeaway due to time pressure. Values convenience over everything. Wants healthy options but won't spend more than 30 mins cooking.

needs →Quick recipes + in-context nutrition so he doesn't have to think
Anna
🩺 health condition

Managing dietary restrictions that make meal planning complicated. Needs to track nutritional content closely but finds the process exhausting and hard to maintain consistently.

needs →Filtered recipes by dietary needs + automatic nutrition tracking
Javina
💸 financial constraint

Eco-conscious and health-aware but budget-limited. Sustainable food options feel financially inaccessible. Frequently buys in bulk, leading to regular food waste before expiry.

needs →Affordable local eco-options + waste management guidance

cat --honest ./retrospective.md
strengths
+Clear feedback after sign-up, building user trust from the very first interaction.
+Scrollable touchscreen design optimised for Echo Show's interface paradigm.
+Strong research base — 10 interviews + 6 observations drove every decision with evidence.
+Aggregated empathy mapping surfaced 3 distinct user archetypes that shaped the whole product direction.
areas for growth
!Lack of voice navigation for users with busy hands while cooking — a significant miss for the platform.
!Unimplemented interactions like a scrollbar, reducing visual feedback clarity.
!Observations missed scenarios like composting — narrowing waste management insight scope.
!Time constraints meant skipping paper prototype testing before moving to mid-fidelity.
